# Product Prioritization Without Reliable Impact Signals

## The Problem

Product prioritization is performed
without reliable, timely impact signals.

Backlog decisions are made based on intent,
urgency, or stakeholder pressure,
while actual effects become visible too late
to influence prioritization meaningfully.

Progress is visible.
Impact is not.

---

## Why This Happens

- Outcomes are delayed and hard to attribute
- Metrics are detached from decisions
- Experiments are expensive or slow
- Feedback loops depend on integration or release cadence
- Learning is not treated as a required outcome

Prioritization optimizes confidence, not evidence.

---

## Observable Symptoms

- Backlog items complete without clear impact
- Reprioritization causes frustration and rework
- Stakeholder discussions focus on status
- Product success is explained retrospectively
- Opportunity cost remains invisible

Product ownership becomes defensive.

---

## Why This Blocks Fast Delivery and Learning

Without early impact signals:

- wrong assumptions scale
- learning lags behind delivery
- prioritization becomes political
- innovation is crowded out by commitments

The organization moves fast,
but learns slowly.

---

## Why This Is a Systemic Problem

This cannot be solved by better planning.

It is caused by:
- missing experiment infrastructure
- lack of attribution context
- separation of delivery and measurement

As long as impact is not observable early,
product decisions remain opinions.
