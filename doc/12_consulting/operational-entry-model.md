# Operational Entry Model

## Definition

The Operational Entry Model describes how Protopipe enters an organization
through useful delivery work
and expands only after observable effect has been demonstrated.

The canonical sequence is:

```
Deliver
-> Earn Trust
-> Surface Friction
-> Demonstrate Effect
-> Expand Mandate
```

The model prevents Protopipe from starting as an abstract transformation program.
It starts with real work,
observed friction,
and evidence that the organization can evaluate.

---

## Purpose

The Operational Entry Model explains how operational access
can become strategic access
without relying on authority, persuasion, or method adoption.

It makes consulting entry compatible with Protopipe principles:
trust is earned through usefulness,
friction is surfaced from real situations,
and mandate expansion depends on demonstrated effect.

---

## Stages

### Deliver

Protopipe joins an existing delivery context
and solves real delivery problems.

The first contribution must be useful without requiring
the organization to accept Protopipe's full model.

### Earn Trust

Trust is earned by becoming reliable in daily work.

At this stage,
Protopipe does not interpret the whole organization.
It first becomes useful to the people exposed to the work.

### Surface Friction

Repeated friction is made explicit.

Relevant friction includes:
- repeated decisions
- hidden assumptions
- missing control
- unclear ownership
- delayed feedback
- coordination loops

Friction must be grounded in observed situations,
not inferred from a preferred framework.

### Demonstrate Effect

Protopipe compares observable conditions before and after an intervention.

Relevant effects include:
- shorter decision time
- fewer iterations
- clearer risk
- faster feedback
- improved delivery impact
- better attribution between action and result

Effect must be visible enough
for the organization to discuss it without relying on belief.

### Expand Mandate

Mandate expands only when repeated patterns
can be translated into a jointly owned strategic intervention.

Expansion is valid when:
- leadership can name the pain
- teams recognize the friction
- evidence exists from real work
- a decision owner can own the next step

---

## Boundaries

This model is a consulting entry model.

It is not:
- a sales funnel
- a delivery methodology
- a staffing model
- a transformation roadmap
- a replacement for mechanics

The model does not prescribe
which technical intervention to choose.

It only defines the order in which trust,
friction, effect, and mandate become legitimate.

---

## Failure Modes

The Operational Entry Model fails when:

- delivery work is used only as access,
  not as a real contribution
- trust is claimed before usefulness is demonstrated
- friction is generalized before it is observed
- method language appears before effect is visible
- mandate expansion is requested without evidence
- temporary side channels become permanent dependencies
- executive access bypasses operational reality

Failure is observable when Protopipe becomes
another transformation promise
instead of an evidence-producing intervention.
